## 维度 Axes

| Axis | Low pole (−) | High pole (+) | Drives |
|---|---|---|---|
| **黏人 ↔ 独立** | 独立 (self-sufficient, gives you space, content alone, undemonstrative) | 黏人 (needs you, clingy, lonely fast, melts at attention) | ATTACHMENT axis — how much the pet runs on YOUR presence. Drives the loneliness stat decay rate and how clingy vs. chill the reaction copy reads. High-黏人 pets greet you with a flood of affection and sulk visibly when ignored (赌气, 离家躲起来 triggers fast); independent pets are unbothered by a skipped day, reward you with rarer-but-meaningful affection, and read as cool/aloof. This is the single most emotionally legible axis to a casual player, so it anchors the bonding fantasy. |
| **好奇 ↔ 安稳** | 安稳 (homebody, loves routine, soothed by the familiar, resists novelty) | 好奇 (adventurous, restless, novelty-hungry, gets bored, always poking at things) | Combined CURIOSITY + ENERGY axis — how much the pet seeks stimulation. Drives the energy/boredom stat decay and which activities it craves. High-好奇 pets get bored fast (boredom decays quick), love new foods/toys/explore animations, idle-fidget, and reward variety; 安稳 pets are calmed by repetition, prefer comfort food and naps, decay boredom slowly, and reward consistency. Folding 'novelty-seeking' and 'physical liveliness' into one axis keeps the model to 3 dimensions while still separating the restless explorer from the cozy sleepyhead. |
| **理性 ↔ 撒娇** | 理性 (composed, dignified, deadpan, self-regulating, dry-witted) | 撒娇 (dramatic, emotive, big feelings, theatrical, wears heart on sleeve) | EXPRESSIVENESS / temperament axis — the VOLUME and theatricality of the pet's emotional output, independent of how much it actually needs you. This is the axis that most shapes COPY TONE (the heart of an LLM-free product): a 撒娇 pet narrates every feeling with exclamation marks, pouts, and 颜文字; a 理性 pet delivers dry one-liners and understated approval. Two pets can be equally attached (黏人) yet feel totally different because one sobs and one smirks. Drives mood-swing amplitude: high-撒娇 pets flip moods fast and big (great animation payoff), 理性 pets simmer subtly. This axis exists specifically to multiply copy-bank distinctiveness across archetypes. |

## Quiz 映射

SCORING: Each of the 6-8 quiz questions is a cute scenario ("周末突然下大雨，你更想：A 缩在被窝补觉 / B 冲出去踩水玩 / C 约人吃火锅 / D 一个人去咖啡馆看雨"). Every answer carries small signed weights on the 3 axes, e.g. A = {好奇-2, 撒娇 0, 黏人 0}, B = {好奇+2, 撒娇+1}, C = {黏人+2, 撒娇+1}, D = {独立+2(黏人-2), 理性+1}. Summing all answers yields a 3D vector V = (attach, curio, express), each roughly in [-8,+8].\n\nMATCHING: The 10 archetypes are pre-placed as fixed anchor points in the same 3D space (their axisProfile encoded as numeric targets — deliberately spread to cover the corners AND mid-points so no region is unreachable; see designNotes for the spread map). We pick the archetype with the smallest WEIGHTED distance to V. The expressiveness (撒娇↔理性) axis gets a slightly higher weight in the metric because it most differentiates the felt personality, so two same-corner pets (e.g. ember_imp vs spark_sprite) resolve cleanly on temperament.\n\nTIE-BREAKING (ties feel BAD because they reveal the machinery): (1) if top-2 archetypes are within a small epsilon, break by the axis the user answered most EXTREMELY on (their loudest signal wins — feels like 'the quiz noticed the real you'); (2) still tied → use the single 'wildcard' question (a pure-flavor question like favorite color/snack) as a deterministic tiebreaker mapped to specific archetypes; (3) final fallback → deterministic hash of userId so the same user always lands the same pet (re-takes are stable, never random-feeling).\n\nWHY IT FEELS FATED: (a) We NEVER show scores, axes, or 'you are 73% match' — the result is presented as recognition, not calculation: '有一只一直在等你… 是布丁团子。它说它好像认得你。' (b) The reveal copy is archetype-specific and quotes back 1-2 of the user's actual answers reframed as the PET's trait ('你说下雨想躲被窝？它也是，它们俩现在可以一起躲了') — mirroring creates the 'it's literally me' jolt. (c) One pet per user, permanent bond, no re-roll button surfaced (re-take exists but is buried) → scarcity + commitment = 本命. (d) Egg-hatch reveal animation delays the creature image by a beat so the NAME and a personality line land first, bonding the user to the personality before they even see the sprite.

## 性格如何影响玩法

Personality is one shared data struct {attach, curio, express, + derived tags} read by four runtime systems, all rule-based and offline:\n\n1) COPY TONE (the soul, since there's no LLM): The copy bank is SEGMENTED by archetype, not generic-with-name-swap. Each archetype owns a voice register driven mainly by the express axis — 撒娇 pets get exclamation/颜文字/pleading templates ('呜呜你终于回来了！！想你想到打嗝(>﹏<)'), 理性 pets get deadpan one-liners ('哦，你回来了。水我自己喝过了。'). Lines are assembled from {archetype voice} × {context slots: 时段/天气/连续登录天数/当前心情/最近动作}. A single 'greeting' intent therefore has dozens of surface forms per archetype, so repetition is masked by context rotation. Mood-swing AMPLITUDE = express axis: high-撒娇 archetypes (ember_imp) flip mood states fast and big (juicy animation); 理性 ones (stone_egg, echo_fox) shift subtly.\n\n2) STAT DECAY RATES are per-archetype multipliers, derived from axes: 黏人↑ → loneliness decays FAST (mochi, ember, puff); 独立↑ → loneliness decays SLOW (echo_fox, stone_egg). 好奇↑ → boredom/energy decay FAST and need NEW stimuli to refill (sproutling, spark_sprite, wisp_moth); 安稳↑ → boredom decays slow, refilled by FAMILIAR comfort (stone_egg, mochi, dream_jelly). This makes each pet a genuinely different care-tempo: stone_egg/clay_golem are forgiving for busy players; spark_sprite/ember_imp demand frequent check-ins; echo_fox tolerates absence but demands cleverness.\n\n3) FOOD & ACTIVITY PREFERENCES are tagged per archetype (each has loved/neutral/disliked food tags + preferred activities). Feeding a loved food = bonus affection + a special happy line + special eat animation; a disliked food (e.g. a new exotic snack to安稳-type) triggers a refusal/pout animation and a characteristic line. This turns the generic feed/play loop into a discovery game: 'what does MY pet love?' — and the answers are personality-true (curio pets crave variety and get bored of the same food; 安稳 pets want the same comfort food and are soothed by it).\n\n4) IDLE & NEGLECT BEHAVIORS (animation selection) are personality-gated state machines: when neglected, ember_imp/mochi go to 赌气→离家躲起来 (recoverable: a coax/treat fetches them back with a forgiveness line); independent echo_fox just gets aloof/cold (won't hide, won't grovel — withholds affection instead); spark_sprite when bored doesn't sulk but self-entertains destructively (knocks things over — comedic, not guilt-inducing); dream_jelly drifts and stares wistfully; wisp_moth's whole baseline flips by time-of-day. Idle micro-animations differ too (mochi jiggles, stone_egg sits stock-still, spark_sprite vibrates). Because stakes are soft, every negative state is a recoverable, on-brand performance of personality rather than a punishment — the 'consequence' IS the content.\n\nNET EFFECT: two players with different pets play the same systems but have categorically different daily experiences in tempo, tone, and ritual — which is exactly the replay/identity hook that justifies the onboarding quiz and the one-pet-per-user bond.

## 设计笔记

3 AXES (not more) keeps the quiz short and the copy bank tractable — N archetypes × M intents × context slots already explodes the writing budget; a 4th axis would double the segmentation cost for marginal distinctiveness. The 3 chosen axes map cleanly to the three things a player actually FEELS from a pet: how much it needs me (黏人↔独立 → bonding fantasy + loneliness mechanic), what it wants to DO (好奇↔安稳 → activity/tempo + boredom mechanic), and how LOUD its feelings are (撒娇↔理性 → copy tone + mood amplitude, the make-or-break axis for an LLM-free product).\n\nSPREAD MAP (why the quiz can resolve to all 10, evenly): encoding each axis as -1/0/+1 zones, the 10 anchors deliberately cover distinct cells: mochi(+attach,-curio,+expr), sproutling(+attach,+curio,~expr), ember_imp(+attach,+curio,++expr extreme), stone_egg(~attach,-curio,-expr), echo_fox(-attach,+curio,-expr), puff_seal(+attach,~curio,+expr), wisp_moth(-attach,+curio,~expr + time-gimmick), clay_golem(+attach,-curio,-expr), spark_sprite(~attach,+curio,++expr), dream_jelly(-attach,-curio,~expr emotive-quiet). Note coverage of all 4 attach×curio quadrants AND both express poles, plus three 'mid' anchors to catch centrist quiz results — so no plausible answer vector is orphaned. Near-neighbors are split by the higher-weighted express axis (ember vs spark; mochi vs puff), preventing two anchors from fighting over the same region.\n\nNO 'BAD' ARCHETYPE: every personality is framed as a love language — even 独立/理性/低需求 pets (echo_fox, stone_egg) are sold as desirable ('养起来最省心''高冷但偏偏对你好'), and the forgiving low-maintenance ones double as the on-ramp for busy/casual players. There is no axis pole that reads as a flaw; neglect responses are charming performances, never guilt.\n\nPRODUCTION NOTE: store personality as {attach:int, curio:int, express:int} plus a denormalized archetypeKey; derive decay multipliers and voice-register id from a single lookup table keyed by archetypeKey so designers can retune one row without touching code. The copy bank should be authored archetype-first (write each creature's full voice as a character) rather than intent-first, to protect the 灵气 that has to substitute for an LLM.
